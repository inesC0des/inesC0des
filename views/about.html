Hi! I'm Ines, a biologist born in northern Germany.


My passions lie in science, education and technology. After school, I decided to delve deep into science, earning a PhD in malaria research from Columbia University. Later on, I went into project management for a non-profit in the field of malaria vaccine development. And after that, I helped building up an interdisciplinary, multi-locational graduate program in bioeconomy.

Currently, my day job is creating the "Book of the Future" at a big science publisher. And I am learning how to program at night (and early morning).


This website is about my coding projects, some UX and UI work that I have done and my journey of learning how to code.

My interest in coding started in elementary school when my father brought home a Want 286. I first copied BASICS code from Kellogg's Frosties packages into DOS to create little hangman programs, then searched the local library for "coding for kids" books to learn a bit more. I continued exploring on the old HP CX programmamble calculator my uncle gave to me, and later informatics classes learning a bit of Turbo Pascal and HTML at high school (I even made it into the local newspaper for being the only girl in the first informatics class at my school).


After a bit of a hiatus (of 15 years) I picked up coding again and started with learning Swift as a hobby in 2014. I also got into fullstack web development and had a peak into Python.

I've been continuously learning and applying these skills in various projects, expecially in creating user-friendly and impactful digital content. My focus has always been on making learning engaging and accessible, particularly in the realms of science and technology.

Welcome to my world, where my excitement of biology meets my passion for education and the creativity of programming, all aimed at creating a more informed and connected digital universe.
